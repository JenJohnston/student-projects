Health and Nutrition

Ancient Origins

Known in China as "The Great Yellow" Rhubarb has been used for its medicinal properties for thousands of years. Rhubarb made an appearance in The Divine Farmer's Herb-Root Classic, an almost 2000 year old manual on Chinese pharmacology. Rhubarb was commonly used as a laxative and to reduce fever's even though no clinical evidence exists that it has any effect.

Eventually, due to it's high prices and increasing demand from apothecaries, traders such as Marco Polo found a way to cultivate rhubarb in Europe.

Medicinal Roots

According to Dr. Mao, rhubarb roots are used as an astringent tonic for the digestive system and in larger doses, used as a laxative. Rhubarb may also treat liver and gall bladder conditions, hemorrhoids and gastritis as well as several skin conditions.
